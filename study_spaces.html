Study Spaces at UMSI

Study Spaces Overview

The University of Michigan offers a variety of study spaces designed to support both individual and group work. Whether you prefer quiet, distraction-free zones for focused study or collaborative rooms for group projects, you'll find spaces that cater to your needs. In addition, UMSI provides access to online resources and virtual study groups, allowing you to study flexibly and connect with peers remotely.

Quiet Study Areas

There is a variety of quiet spaces across campus where students can study independently without distractions. These spaces are ideal for those who need to focus on coursework, research, or reading. The University Library system provides access to dedicated quiet zones equipped with comfortable seating, power outlets, and ample natural light. The UMSI building includes quiet study areas designed to foster concentration and productivity.

Group Study Spaces

For collaborative projects, there are several group study spaces available. These spaces are designed to accommodate teams of students working together on assignments, presentations, or group discussions. Group study rooms are equipped with whiteboards, media equipment, and large tables to create and help with collaboration. Some spaces are available on a first-come, first-served basis, while others can be reserved in advance via the University room's booking system.

Online Study Resources

In addition to physical study spaces, UMSI students also have access to a range of online resources to support their academic needs. The University Library's digital collections offer access to e-books, academic papers, and online databases that are available 24/7. Online study groups and virtual tutoring services also provide students with remote support.
