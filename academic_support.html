Academic Support at UMSI

Overview of Academic Support

At the University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI), we are committed to supporting our students' academic success by offering a wide array of resources and services. Our academic advising team provides personalized guidance to help you navigate your curriculum, select courses, and address any academic challenges or questions you may encounter during your studies. UMSI students also have access to the extensive collections and study spaces within the University Library system, offering both digital and physical resources essential for research and coursework. Additionally, our specialized tutoring services and the UMSI Writing Center are available to support you in mastering complex subjects and enhancing your written communication skills. By utilizing these academic resources, UMSI students can thrive in their studies and achieve their educational goals.

Academic Advising Services

Our academic advisors are dedicated to helping you succeed throughout your time at UMSI. They offer personalized support, including guidance on course selection, navigating program requirements, and exploring career opportunities. Do not be afraid to reach out, as our advisors are here to help you plan a path to success!

Writing Center and Tutoring Services

The UMSI Writing Center provides one-on-one tutoring to help students with writing tasks, from research papers to presentations. Whether you need assistance with structuring your ideas, editing drafts, or improving your writing skills, the Writing Center is available to support your academic work.

Additionally, subject-specific tutors are available to help students tackle challenging coursework in areas such as programming, data analysis, and theoretical subjects. These tutors are available by appointment and can provide targeted help to enhance your understanding of difficult topics.

Tips for Advising Appointment

To make the most of your academic advising experience, here are some tips:

  • 1. Choose the Right Service:
    • 1:1 Appointments: Schedule in advance for detailed questions (15-30 minutes).
    • Drop-in Advising: No appointment needed; great for quick, time-sensitive questions (first-come, first-served).
    • Email: Ideal for asynchronous communication. Expect a reply within 24 hours.
  • 2. Be Aware of Deadlines: Keep track of key dates like add/drop deadlines and financial aid deadlines. Schedule appointments early to avoid last-minute issues. Reach out before a problem becomes urgent.
  • 3. Prepare for Your Appointment: Set clear goals for the meeting, review your Degree Audit and Transcript, and make a list of questions. Be on time and avoid distractions during virtual meetings.
  • 4. Follow Through: Complete any action steps suggested by your advisor (e.g., registering for classes, contacting other offices). Reach out if you need further assistance.
